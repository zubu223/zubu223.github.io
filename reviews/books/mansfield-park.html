(DNF) sweet home alabamashire

this is my introduction to jane austen's writing, and while the author is clearly very skilled this story is not good.
i don't really care that there isn't much plot, that's fine and to be expected with the genre, but these characters are just honk shooooooo mimimimimimi
the plot that is there revolves around this poor, sickly girl named fanny (hah) who loves her first cousin, some guy named edmund.

while cousin-fucking was totally normal in miss austen's time, this really messes up the entire story. terribly so. i cannot root for this girl to marry her cousin dude.
it doesn't help that the villain, mary crawford, is a much more interesting character and notably, not edmunds cousin. this flips the entire narrative on it's head, where you root for the villain and are disgusted when the hero succeeds.

it's a DNF for me. i do not particularly care to read any more about the goings on in incester, alabamashire.
not a good introduction to the only 19th century author to be featured in Saints Row IV.
