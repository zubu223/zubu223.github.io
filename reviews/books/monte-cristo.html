The Count of Monte Cristo

1844

Alexandre Dumas

12-20-2025

holy peak

this book is very long. that being said, it is an excellent book that will not fail to keep you engaged at any point.
the plot is great, the characters are well defined and everyone is a little bit insane. it might even make you *shudder* feel some kind of endearment towards france.

the protagonist, edmond dantes is a wonderful character and the prototypical "good man pushed too far" cliche protagonist, except not just a cheap power-fantasy self insert.
dantes is a very insane person (anyone would be after what he goes through) who by chance or divine providence gains a hell of a lot of money. the guy is ridiculously rich.

diamonds and gemstones just spew from his fingertips, he hands them out like candy and dantes LOVES to flex. so much he invents a new persona as the count of an island with no inhabitants.
if he lived in 2025 he would be a hypebeast wearing supreme and gucci and he would dazzle his house guests with platters of dubai chocolate and 24k gold labubus.

VERDICT:
10/10 BOOK
read it someday or watch the movie or something because this story is excellent.
